OpenMessages

The open-source, AI-native messaging client. Investment thesis and business plan.

The opportunity

Messaging is fragmented. People use 3-5 messaging apps daily. Every attempt to unify them has been closed-source, cloud-dependent, and expensive. None have AI integration.

Automattic acquired Beeper for $125M and Texts.com for $50M — proving the unified messaging market is real. Beeper now charges $9.99/month and has no AI features, no local API, and no developer ecosystem.

Meanwhile, AI assistants are becoming the primary interface for knowledge workers. MCP (Model Context Protocol) is emerging as the standard for connecting AI to tools. No messaging client has native AI/MCP support.

What OpenMessage is

A local-first, open-source messaging client that connects to your messaging services and exposes them to AI assistants via MCP. Currently supports Google Messages (SMS/RCS). Built on the same open-source libraries (mautrix) that power Beeper's $125M product.

Key architectural differences from Beeper:

  • Local-first — messages stay on your machine. No cloud account, no Matrix server, no subscription required for basic use
  • AI-native — built-in MCP server lets any AI assistant read, search, and send messages across all connected services
  • Open source — Apache 2.0. Community-driven development, auditable code, extensible by anyone
  • Native apps — Swift on macOS (not Electron). Planned native apps for all platforms

Business model

Open-source core, commercial premium. Similar to GitLab, Supabase, PostHog.

  • Free tier — open-source app with unlimited messaging services, local MCP server. Community-supported
  • Premium ($10/month) — hosted AI features: smart replies, conversation summaries, priority inbox, natural language search, cross-service context. BYO API key or included credits
  • Team/Enterprise — shared AI context across team communications, compliance features, SSO, admin controls

The free tier is the distribution engine. Users adopt for the unified client, upgrade for AI features. Open source builds trust and community contributions — the same model that made Beeper viable enough for a $125M exit.

Why now

  • MCP adoption is accelerating — Anthropic, OpenAI, and the broader ecosystem are converging on MCP as the standard for AI-tool integration. First-mover in messaging + MCP is a strong position
  • AI assistants are becoming primary interfaces — users increasingly want to say "check my messages" rather than switch apps. The assistant that can actually do this wins
  • Beeper proved the market — $125M acquisition validates unified messaging. But Beeper chose cloud-first and hasn't shipped AI. The local-first + AI opportunity is wide open
  • mautrix libraries are mature — years of development, battle-tested by Beeper at scale. We don't need to reverse-engineer protocols from scratch
